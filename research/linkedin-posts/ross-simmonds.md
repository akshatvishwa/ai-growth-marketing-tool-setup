## Post 1

Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosssimmonds_llmvisibility-aivisibility-reddit-activity-7474812197420990465-RG4r
Date: 5 days ago
Topic: Off-site B2B buying journey, Reddit, YouTube, reviews, and AI visibility

### Summary
- Ross explains that by the time a B2B buyer books a demo, much of the decision has already been made outside the brand’s website.
- Buyers are influenced by third-party sources such as G2 reviews, Reddit threads, YouTube videos, ChatGPT, Claude, review sites, and independent communities.
- These off-site sources shape the buyer shortlist before the prospect fills out a form.
- Ross argues that many of the same sources buyers trust are also used by AI models when building answers.
- He highlights Reddit, YouTube, reviews, and niche creators as important parts of modern B2B visibility.
- His team uses tactics such as real Reddit participation, YouTube optimization, review analysis, and BOFU creator content to influence buying journeys and AI visibility.

### Tactical Takeaway
- B2B SaaS teams should map where their category is discussed and decided, then build visibility in those spaces.
- This includes Reddit, G2, YouTube, review sites, niche communities, comparison content, and AI search prompts.
- Brands should make both owned pages and off-site content easy for humans and AI systems to understand, quote, and cite.

### B2B SaaS Relevance
- This is highly relevant for B2B SaaS because buyers often research software options across review sites, Reddit, YouTube, peer communities, analyst content, and AI tools before requesting a demo.
- SaaS companies that only optimize their own website may miss important decision-making moments happening on third-party platforms.
- The post supports the idea that demand generation, SEO, content marketing, and AI visibility need to work together across the full buying journey.

### AI SEO / Content Relevance
- This post supports the idea that AI SEO is not limited to website content.
- AI-powered SEO content production should include off-site visibility, third-party trust signals, review mining, YouTube optimization, Reddit participation, and citation-ready content.
- It also shows that brands need to monitor prompts and keywords because AI systems can shape perception before users ever visit the website.

### Risk or Limitation
- Participating in communities like Reddit requires authenticity and transparency. Fake engagement, spam, or astroturfing can damage brand trust.
- Off-site visibility is harder to control than owned content, so teams need long-term trust-building rather than short-term promotional tactics.
- Not every B2B SaaS category will have the same Reddit, YouTube, or review-site behavior, so teams should audit their own category before investing heavily.

### Use in Future Playbook
- This source can support a playbook section on off-site AI visibility.
- It can also support workflows for mapping buyer research sources, auditing Reddit and review visibility, optimizing old YouTube videos, analyzing reviews for product insights, and creating BOFU creator-led content.

## Post 2

Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosssimmonds_marketing-aivisibility-contentmarketing-activity-7465397670220713984--Flg
Date: 1 month ago
Topic: EEAT signals, off-site authority, and platform-specific AI visibility

### Summary
- Ross argues that EEAT signals are one of the most important factors for AI visibility.
- He explains that when users ask LLMs a question, AI systems expand that question into related queries and look for evidence that the cited information is valid and trustworthy.
- Brands that focused on expert-driven, research-backed, high-quality content are more likely to be cited by AI systems.
- Ross emphasizes that brands publishing beyond their own websites are winning because LLMs use a wide surface area to build answers.
- He notes that different AI systems may prefer different source types, such as LinkedIn, YouTube, and Reddit.
- His recommendation is to run category-specific analysis and build a marketing playbook rooted in EEAT.

### Tactical Takeaway
- B2B SaaS teams should not guess where AI systems are pulling information from.
- They should run their own AI visibility analysis across important prompts, keywords, competitors, and platforms.
- Based on the findings, they should build expert-driven content and off-site authority on the platforms that matter most in their category.

### B2B SaaS Relevance
- This is relevant for B2B SaaS because software buyers often rely on trust, proof, expertise, comparisons, and third-party validation before choosing a vendor.
- SaaS brands need authority across multiple surfaces, not only their own blog.
- For example, visibility on LinkedIn, YouTube, Reddit, review sites, and trusted industry sources can influence both human buyers and AI-generated recommendations.

### AI SEO / Content Relevance
- This source reinforces that AI SEO depends on more than traditional ranking factors.
- AI-powered SEO content production should be connected to EEAT, expert input, research-backed content, off-site mentions, platform-specific visibility, and citation tracking.
- It also supports the idea that each AI platform may have different source preferences, so one universal strategy may not work.

### Risk or Limitation
- EEAT is difficult to measure directly, so teams need practical proxy metrics such as expert involvement, citations, mentions, reviews, creator coverage, and third-party visibility.
- Platform preferences may change over time, so the strategy needs regular monitoring.
- Teams should avoid treating one study or one platform pattern as a permanent rule.

### Use in Future Playbook
- This source can support a playbook section on EEAT for AI visibility.
- It can also support workflows for AI visibility audits, platform-specific citation research, expert-driven content creation, off-site distribution planning, and category-specific authority building.

## Patterns Observed
- Ross consistently argues that B2B buying decisions now happen across a web of third-party sources, not only on a company website.
- His content connects AI visibility with real buyer behavior, especially Reddit, YouTube, reviews, communities, and AI assistants.
- He emphasizes that off-site authority and content distribution are now critical parts of SEO and AI visibility.
- He treats Reddit, YouTube, LinkedIn, review platforms, and niche communities as strategic surfaces for both human trust and AI citations.
- Ross focuses heavily on EEAT, expert-driven content, and research-backed visibility instead of generic content volume.
- His approach suggests that B2B SaaS teams need to combine owned content, community participation, third-party validation, creator partnerships, and AI prompt monitoring.
- A recurring theme is that brands should run their own category-specific analysis instead of copying generic AI SEO advice.

## Notes for Future Playbook
- Map where the target SaaS category is actually discussed and decided: Reddit, YouTube, G2, review sites, LinkedIn, communities, comparison pages, and AI tools.
- Audit how often the brand and competitors appear in important AI prompts, Reddit discussions, YouTube results, review pages, and category conversations.
- Build transparent and useful participation in third-party communities instead of using fake engagement or promotional spam.
- Optimize existing YouTube videos, webinars, demos, and product explainers because video content can influence both buyers and AI systems.
- Use customer reviews and negative feedback to identify product gaps, support issues, content ideas, and buyer concerns.
- Create BOFU content with niche creators, experts, and trusted voices who already influence the category.
- Make owned and off-site content easy to quote, cite, and understand by clearly answering questions, using expert input, and structuring information well.
- Treat EEAT as a practical system: expert authors, research-backed content, third-party mentions, reviews, creator coverage, community trust, and consistent category visibility.
- Repeat AI visibility analysis regularly because AI citation sources and platform preferences can change over time.
