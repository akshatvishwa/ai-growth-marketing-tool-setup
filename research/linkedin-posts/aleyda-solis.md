## Post 1

Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aleyda_how-to-build-a-representative-ai-search-activity-7469723521947131905-6DVK
Date: 2 weeks ago
Topic: Representative AI search prompt library for visibility measurement

### Summary
- Aleyda explains that one major mistake in AI search tracking is using a non-representative prompt library.
- A random list of prompts or a tool’s default prompt set can give an inaccurate view of a brand’s AI search presence.
- A useful prompt library should represent real AI-assisted customer journeys across products, audiences, markets, journey stages, competitors, and buyer constraints.
- The goal is not to track every possible prompt, but to measure the right prompts well enough to identify patterns, diagnose visibility gaps, and guide optimization actions.
- The post outlines a framework for defining what the prompt library should represent, grouping prompts, localizing prompts, measuring visibility defensibly, and turning findings into action.

### Tactical Takeaway
- B2B SaaS teams should create a structured AI search prompt library based on actual buyer journeys instead of relying on random prompts or default tool-generated prompt lists.
- The prompt library should include category prompts, use-case prompts, competitor prompts, pricing prompts, comparison prompts, audience-specific prompts, and market-specific prompts.

### B2B SaaS Relevance
- This is highly relevant for B2B SaaS because SaaS buyers often research across multiple stages before converting: problem awareness, category exploration, vendor comparison, pricing, alternatives, integrations, and implementation concerns.
- A representative prompt library can help SaaS teams understand whether they appear in the right AI-assisted buying journeys.
- It can also help identify where competitors are being recommended more often and where the brand is missing from important decision-stage prompts.

### AI SEO / Content Relevance
- This supports the idea that AI SEO needs measurement systems, not just content production.
- AI-powered content should be planned around real prompts, buyer questions, use cases, competitive comparisons, and decision criteria.
- The post also connects AI visibility measurement with optimization actions, making it useful for turning research into a repeatable SEO workflow.

### Risk or Limitation
- A prompt library can become biased if it is based only on internal assumptions instead of real buyer language.
- Teams should validate prompts using search data, sales calls, CRM notes, customer support questions, reviews, community discussions, and competitor research.
- AI search results can vary by tool, location, language, and time, so measurement should be repeated regularly.

### Use in Future Playbook
- This source can support a playbook section on building a representative AI search prompt library.
- It can also support workflows for AI visibility audits, competitor visibility tracking, prompt grouping, buyer journey mapping, and turning AI search findings into content priorities.

## Patterns Observed
- Aleyda’s content focuses heavily on practical AI search measurement rather than vague AI SEO advice.
- She emphasizes that AI visibility should be measured through structured prompt libraries, not random prompt testing.
- Her approach connects SEO strategy with real buyer journeys, markets, audience segments, competitors, and decision-stage questions.
- AI search is treated as both a visibility channel and a measurement challenge, not only as a source of website traffic.
- Traditional SEO metrics like rankings and clicks are becoming incomplete because AI answers and zero-click experiences can influence buyer decisions without generating immediate sessions.
- AI search strategy requires a mix of content quality, technical accessibility, structured information, citation visibility, competitor comparison, and ongoing monitoring.
- Her weekly update format shows that AI search is changing quickly, so SEO teams need a regular process for tracking important platform changes.

## Notes for Future Playbook
- Build a representative AI search prompt library before measuring AI visibility.
- Group prompts by product, audience, use case, market, buyer journey stage, competitor, and constraint.
- Use AI visibility measurement to identify patterns, gaps, competitor advantages, and optimization opportunities.
- Track more than rankings and organic clicks. Include recommendation rate, citation presence, competitor visibility, prompt coverage, and representation accuracy.
- Create content that answers real buyer questions across the full decision journey, especially category, comparison, pricing, alternative, integration, and use-case prompts.
- Monitor AI search updates regularly, but prioritize only the changes that affect visibility, citations, buyer behavior, or reporting.
- Combine content strategy with technical SEO and structured information so AI systems can access, understand, and cite the brand accurately.
