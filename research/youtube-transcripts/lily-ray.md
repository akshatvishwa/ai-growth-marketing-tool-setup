## Summary

* Lily explains that AI Overviews are difficult to measure perfectly because Google Search Console does not provide full visibility into how users interact with AI Overview citations.
* She recommends using AI Overview tracking tools to understand which websites are cited and how AI-generated answers appear for important queries.
* She explains that AI Overviews can reduce traffic in many cases, but some brands may receive more visibility when they are mentioned or cited multiple times inside the AI-generated result.
* Lily says many SEO fundamentals still matter, including valuable content, crawlability, indexability, and rendering.
* However, she also says brands need to monitor how AI Overviews, AI Mode, ChatGPT, and Perplexity talk about their core topics, questions, and brand.
* She frames AI search as a new form of SEO where brand visibility and mentions can be valuable even when the user does not click.
* Lily discusses E-E-A-T as something that cannot be reduced to a simple checklist or score.
* She emphasizes that real expertise and trust should be built through expert visibility, personal branding, podcasts, conference appearances, industry mentions, academic or professional references, and broader marketing around genuine expertise.

## Tactical Takeaway

* B2B SaaS teams should track how AI Overviews and AI tools describe their brand, category, experts, and core product questions.
* Teams should not only measure clicks and rankings. They should also measure citations, brand mentions, visibility inside AI answers, and whether the brand is represented accurately.
* Companies should strengthen E-E-A-T by making real internal experts more visible across trusted third-party surfaces, not only by adding author pages or schema markup.

## B2B SaaS Relevance

* This is highly relevant for B2B SaaS because SaaS buyers often evaluate trust, authority, category expertise, and product credibility before speaking with sales.
* Many SaaS companies have strong internal experts who are not visible enough outside the company website.
* These experts can become stronger trust signals through podcasts, webinars, conference talks, industry publications, LinkedIn posts, partner content, and third-party mentions.
* B2B SaaS teams should treat expert visibility as both an SEO and brand-building activity.
* AI search may mention brands without sending immediate clicks, so SaaS teams need to value visibility, mentions, and authority signals in addition to website traffic.

## Risk or Limitation

* AI Overview visibility is difficult to measure perfectly because reporting tools and Google Search Console do not provide complete data.
* AI-generated answers can still hallucinate or cite low-authority sources, so teams should avoid assuming every AI citation is a quality signal.
* E-E-A-T cannot be solved with a simple checklist, schema markup, or author bio alone.
* Brands should avoid fake expertise or artificial authority-building tactics because trust needs to be earned through real experience, evidence, and public credibility.
