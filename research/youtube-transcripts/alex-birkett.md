## Summary

* Alex explains that Omniscient works on SEO, content marketing, digital PR, and organic growth for B2B brands, with AI search becoming a major part of that work.
* He says business buyers are increasingly using LLMs at the earliest touchpoints in the customer journey, so brands need to show up before users ever visit their website.
* The biggest shift is not only tactical SEO. It is a shift in consumer behavior: users expect AI systems to synthesize information from websites, competitors, Reddit, YouTube, and other sources into one answer.
* Alex argues that much GEO/AEO advice sounds similar to good SEO: structured content, clear headers, natural language, listicles, and bottom-of-funnel content.
* The strategic starting point is to define the competitive landscape: category entry points, buyer pain points, and the prompts that matter for the business.
* Instead of tracking only keyword rankings, brands should measure how often they are mentioned in AI outputs across ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and other tools.
* After measuring baseline visibility, teams should analyze the citation sources behind AI answers to find gaps where they need owned content, third-party mentions, listicle inclusion, Reddit visibility, or other off-site coverage.
* Alex also explains that AI search measurement is probabilistic, not deterministic. Because of personalization and user context, brands should think more like brand marketers and track share of voice across prompt clusters.

## Tactical Takeaway

* B2B SaaS teams should build a prompt cluster around their category, pain points, competitors, and buyer questions.
* They should measure how often the brand appears across those prompts instead of relying only on keyword rankings.
* They should inspect the citation sources behind AI answers and identify whether visibility gaps come from missing owned content, third-party listicles, Reddit discussions, YouTube content, or competitor coverage.

## Risk or Limitation

* AI search visibility is probabilistic, so brands cannot expect stable rankings in the same way they expect traditional search rankings.
* Personalization, memory, location, and prompt wording can change AI outputs, making measurement harder.
* Teams should avoid making decisions from one prompt or one AI answer. They need larger prompt clusters and repeated measurements.
* Citation-source analysis can show opportunity areas, but influencing training data directly is difficult and usually a long-term effort.
